# FAQs for opportunity insights feature in Outlook

These frequently asked questions (FAQs) describe the AI impact of the Sales agent's opportunity insights feature in Outlook.

## What is opportunity insights feature in Outlook?

Opportunity insights is a capability that exists in Outlook today provides insights on an opportunity based on all the insights from activities such as emails and meetings.

## What are the feature's capabilities?

This feature provides insights on an opportunity when the user saves the email to CRM in Outlook. In the **Sales** pane in Outlook, this card provides insights such as what's happening, what's upcoming and potential risks identified across all emails and meetings for a given opportunity. The emails or meetings themselves don't need to reside in CRM for insights to be generated and categorized.

## What is the feature's intended use?

The intended purpose of this feature is to deliver aggregated, high-value insights for sellers by synthesizing information from emails and meetings related to an opportunity. Today, sellers often need to navigate across multiple tools and data sources to piece together critical context and identify actionable insights, which can be both time-consuming and inefficient.

With Sales agent integrated directly into Outlook, these fragmented data points are consolidated and transformed into meaningful, derived insights in a single, familiar workspace. By surfacing contextual recommendations, key signals, and opportunity-specific intelligence, the feature enables sellers to quickly understand the current state of an opportunity, prioritize next steps, and drive progress more effectively.

Ultimately, this capability enhances seller productivity by reducing information overload, minimizing context switching, and empowering smarter, faster decision-making directly within their daily workflow.


## How was the opportunity insights feature evaluated? What metrics are used to measure performance?

The feature is evaluated through a combination of comparative analysis, human review, and customer engagement metrics. Performance is measured based on criteria such as accuracy, relevance, engagement, and customer satisfaction. Response rates gauge the effectiveness of follow-up emails.

End-users provide ongoing feedback on each Copilot feature, along with iterative improvements contribute to optimizing the system's performance across all features.

## What are the limitations of this feature? How can users minimize the impact of the limitations when using the system?

The feature can generate insights from emails and meetings for both the ones that are saved to CRM and the ones that are not saved. In order to see the card itself in the **Sales** pane in Outlook, the email needs to be saved to an opportunity. Without saving, the card will not show up in the **Sales** pane.
